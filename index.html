Recent Work

Mitigating Advanced Adversarial Attacks with More Advanced Gradient Obfuscation Techniques

We focused on deflecting the underlying assumption of advanced interactive attacks, namely the BPDA and EOT attack. We proposed a framework that combines a superior gradient shaffling procedure with a nondifferentiable method to defend adversarial attacks for deep learning models. This figure summarizes the overview of adopting a preprocessing-only defense and the result we attaint. We managed to maintain the model accuracy around 70%, while the BPDA attack invalidates most state-of-art methods. I proposed the gradient obfuscation method, Random Distortion over Grids, and in charge of all the experiments in this work. (Under review by NeurIPS 2020)

Data Augmentation as Defenses on Adversarial Examples: A Comprehensive Case Study

We developed an open-sourced defense framework, termed Fencebox, which currently includes fifteen preprocessing-only defense methods. All the listed methodologies can disturb the input without harm much over the classification result, thus can remove several adversarial perturbations without a high overhead. We encourage researchers to adapt our work and combine existing obfuscation techniques to defeat advanced adversarial attacks better. In this work, eleven methods were proposed or first adopted by us as a defense toward adversarial attacks. A though evaluation and case study are presented in this work.
